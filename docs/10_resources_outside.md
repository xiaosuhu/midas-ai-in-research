# External AI Resources for Research

## 1. Overview
The global AI community provides a wide range of open tools, datasets, and learning resources that can accelerate research. This chapter focuses on **carefully selected, high-value resources** that are widely used, well maintained, and directly useful for academic research.

These resources are grouped by purpose: learning, datasets, modeling tools, compute, and software ecosystems.

---

## 2. Learning & Training Resources

### 2.1 DeepLearning.AI (Andrew Ng)
A leading provider of high-quality AI courses created by Andrew Ng and collaborators.  
Covers:
- Machine learning foundations  
- Deep learning specialization  
- LLMs, prompt engineering, RAG  
- Applied courses (vision, NLP, RL)

**Link:** https://www.deeplearning.ai/

---

### 2.2 Kaggle Learn
Short, hands-on micro-courses for:
- Python  
- Pandas  
- Machine learning  
- Deep learning  
- Computer vision  
- Time series  
- LLM applications  

A great starting point for researchers with limited coding experience.

**Link:** https://www.kaggle.com/learn

---

### 2.3 The Turing Way
An open-source, community-driven handbook for:
- Reproducible research  
- Documentation and project management  
- Ethics, governance, and open science  

Excellent for researchers building responsible and transparent workflows.

**Link:** https://the-turing-way.netlify.app/

---

### 2.4 Fast.ai
Free courses emphasizing practical deep learning and strong intuition.

Focus areas:
- Deep learning from first principles  
- NLP, vision, tabular DL  
- Model interpretation and ethics

**Link:** https://www.fast.ai/

---

### 2.5 *Dive into Deep Learning (D2L)*  
A fully open-source deep learning textbook with runnable code in Jupyter notebooks.  
Widely used in academia for:
- Mathematical foundations of deep learning  
- Practical implementations (PyTorch, MXNet)  
- Vision, NLP, optimization, sequence models  
- Hands-on, experiment-ready examples  

D2L is ideal for researchers who want both theory and executable models.

**Link:** https://d2l.ai/

---

### 2.6 Google Machine Learning Crash Course
A well-structured introduction to machine learning fundamentals.

**Link:** https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/crash-course

---

## 3. Open Datasets & Searchable Repositories

### 3.1 Kaggle Datasets
The single largest collection of queryable datasets across domains.  
- Tabular, image, text, time series  
- Benchmarks with metadata  
- Community notebooks for immediate exploration  

**Link:** https://www.kaggle.com/datasets

---

### 3.2 HuggingFace Datasets
A massive ecosystem of ML-ready datasets with a standardized API.

Covers:
- NLP  
- Vision  
- Audio  
- Multimodal  
- Scientific domains  

**Link:** https://huggingface.co/datasets

---

### 3.3 UCI Machine Learning Repository
Classic datasets ideal for rapid prototyping.

**Link:** https://archive.ics.uci.edu/

---

### 3.4 PhysioNet
Biomedical and physiological datasets, including synthetic and demo sets.

**Link:** https://physionet.org/

---

### 3.5 OpenNeuro
Open neuroimaging datasets (fMRI, EEG, MEG, fNIRS).

**Link:** https://openneuro.org/

---

## 5. Cloud Compute & Low-Cost GPU Resources

### 5.1 Google Colab
Free or low-cost notebooks with GPU/TPU access.  
Great for ML prototyping without local setup.

**Link:** https://colab.research.google.com/

---

### 5.2 Kaggle Notebooks
Free GPU notebooks with zero configuration.

**Link:** https://www.kaggle.com/code

---

### 5.3 Vast.ai
Low-cost GPU rental marketplace for large models and training jobs.

**Link:** https://vast.ai/

---

### 5.4 Lambda Labs
Enterprise-grade GPUs for short- or long-term research use.

**Link:** https://lambdalabs.com/

---

### 5.5 RunPod
Simple GPU workspace setup with templates for:
- Jupyter  
- Workspaces  
- Inference servers  
- Stable Diffusion  

**Link:** https://runpod.io/
