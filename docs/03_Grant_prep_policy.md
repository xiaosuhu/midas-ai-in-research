# Grant Preparation and Policy

1. Introduction
Why AI matters for proposal writing: efficiency, consistency, idea expansion.
Institutional concerns: originality, data management, confidentiality.

2. AI for Grant Ideation and Planning
Literature scoping and gap identification.
Drafting Specific Aims or project summaries.
Generating timelines, budgets, or Gantt charts.

3. AI in Proposal Writing
Structuring narrative sections (Significance, Innovation, Approach).
Improving readability and alignment with review criteria.
Language polishing for clarity and impact.

4. Ethical and Policy Considerations
Disclosing AI use in proposals (NSF, NIH, DOE evolving guidance).
Avoiding inclusion of proprietary or unpublished content in public AI tools.
Privacy of collaborator information, biosketch data, and preliminary results.

5. UM and Sponsor Policies

U-M Research Integrity Office guidelines on generative AI.
NIH and NSF statements on “responsible use of AI in proposal preparation.”
Example disclosure language for AI use.

6. Case Example

Mock NIH or NSF summary paragraph refined using AI (before vs after).
Discussion: what improved, what risks emerged.

7. Conclusion

AI as a support system for thinking better, not just writing faster.
